The Anatomy of an AR Avatar Creator
with Tomoyoshi Yamamoto and Shawk Stanberg
Our Vision
"I was alone. Maybe it was my fault" - Tomoyoshi Yamamoto (Developer). Tomoyoshi Yamamoto has been developing a virtual reality program that creates an avatar that moves talks and does almost anything the cloned person would do in real life. "We have reached 60% in terms of progress" - Shawk Stanberg (BETA tester). The project is about to complete and is scheduled to have its first launch on December.
"I didnt have many friends I can depend on, especially when I was in 8th grade... I was totally isolated." Developer Tomo (Tomyoshi Yamamoto) invisions a new kind of network for kids like him to interact. "Its really hard to find where you belong when you have a totally different interest then others". His life, mainly on how he thinks of his life changed when he first experienced VR. "I was surprised on what I would be able to do with this technology. I can make my own world to stay and build my very own shelter".

About the Project
Report from 10/1/16 follows: In this report, we will like to introduce the prototype of our AR Avatar Creator that heavily uses the structure of WebGL and Machine Learning.
The project is running on this link (https://prazma.github.io/augr). The code is saved on GitHub. The while program uses three elements to fit and structure several algorithms through the use of objects and variables. The algorithms will visualize the result through a VR experience by using WebGL and Accelerometer (HTML5 Sensor API).
Geolocation API will be added to let the users interact with the world. ("experiencing kinetic energy is very important for us, humans in the VR world" -Tomoyoshi Yamamoto). "The cloning technology may lead to a new technology of where we humans can interact with people gone from this world." - Beta Tester
